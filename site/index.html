    • Kale Salad with Pecorino and Lemon

      09.28.2017
      From: The Kitchn
      Ingredients:
      • 1 large bunch kale, washed and trimmed of stems
      • 4 ounces Pecorino Romano, grated
      • 2 lemons, juiced
      • 1/2 cup olive oil
      • Kosher salt and fresh black pepper, to taste


      Directions:
      1. Using your thumb and fingers, strip the leaves from the stem of the kale
      2. Roll the remaining stack of de-veined leaves into a tight cigar shape and slice into thin ribbons.
      3. Whisk the lemon juice and olive oil and pour over the kale
      4. Massage the kale, trying to break down any extra-fibrous leaves, for 2-3 minutes
      5. Toss with the pecorino. Taste and season with salt and pepper. Let the salad sit at room temperature for an hour before serving.

    • Kale and Goat Cheese Fritatta Cups

      09.28.2017
      From: The Kitchn
      Ingredients:
      • 2 cups chopped Lacinato kale
      • 1 garlic clove, thinly sliced
      • 3 tablespoons olive oil
      • 1/4 teaspoons red pepper flakes
      • 8 large eggs
      • 1/4 teaspoon salt
      • Dash ground black pepper
      • 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
      • 1/4 cup goat cheese, crumbled


      Directions:
      1. Preheat the oven to 350°F.
      2. Get 2 cups kale, remove the leaves from the kale ribs. Wash and dry the leaves and cut them into 1/2-inch-wide strips.
      3. In a 10-inch nonstick skillet, cook the garlic in 1 tablespoon of oil over medium-high heat for 30 seconds.
      4. Add the kale and red pepper flakes and cook until wilted, 1 to 2 minutes.
      5. In a medium bowl, beat the eggs with the salt and pepper. Add the kale and thyme to the egg mixture.
      6. Using a 12-cup muffin tin, use the remaining 2 tablespoons of oil to grease 8 of the cups (you may also use butter or non-stick spray if you’d prefer). Sprinkle the tops with goat cheese.
      7. Bake until they are set in the center, about 25 to 30 minutes.

      Frittata is best eaten warm from the oven or within the next day, but leftovers can be kept refrigerated and reheated for up to a week.


    • Broccoli Kale Caesar

      09.28.2017
      From: The Kitchn
      Ingredients:
      • 1 large head broccoli (about 1 pound), cut into florets
      • 6 small cloves garlic, unpeeled
      • 1 medium bunch kale (about 12 ounces), stems removed and leaves coarsely torn
      • 1/4 cup tightly packed fresh parsley leaves, coarsely chopped
      • 1/4 cup toasted sunflower seeds
      • 1 cup cashews, soaked in 1 cup of boiling water for 2 to 8 hours
      • 3/4 cup low-sodium vegetable broth or water
      • 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
      • 2 teaspoons freshly squeezed lemon juice
      • Kosher salt
      • Freshly ground black pepper


      Directions:
      1. Soak the cashews in hot water for an hour
      2. Arrange a rack in the middle of the oven and heat to 400°F.
      3. Place the broccoli florets and unpeeled garlic cloves on a baking sheet.
      4. Drizzle with 11/2 tablespoons of the oil, toss to combine, and spread into an even layer.
      5. Roast until the broccoli is just starting to turn golden-brown, 15 to 20 minutes. Season with salt and set aside.
      6. Squeeze out the garlic from their skins into a high-powered blender. Drain the water from the cashews and add to the blender.
      7. Add the broth or water, oil, and lemon juice. Blend on high speed until very smooth (if it is too thick, blend in a few more splashes of water). Taste and season well with salt and pepper; set aside.
      8. Place the kale, remaining 1 tablespoon of oil, and a big pinch of salt in a large bowl.
      9. Massage with your hands to soften the leaves, then let sit for 5 minutes.
      10. Add the roasted broccoli and parsley and toss to combine. Add the dressing and toss to combine. Sprinkle with the sunflower seeds.
